Under the Wkb, every contractor working on a gevolgklasse 1 building must file three mandatory notifications with the municipality. Missing any of them can stop your project. This guide walks through the timeline, required documents, and how BimDossier keeps you on track.

## The three meldingen

### 1. Bouwmelding — 4 weeks before construction starts

The bouwmelding notifies the municipality that construction will begin. It must include:

- The borgingsplan from your kwaliteitsborger
- The risicobeoordeling (risk assessment)
- Proof of a contracted kwaliteitsborger with an approved instrument
- Project details (address, building type, planned start date)

**Deadline**: at least 4 weeks before the planned start date. Filed via the Omgevingsloket.

### 2. Informatieplicht — 2 working days before start

A shorter notification confirming that all conditions from the bouwmelding are met and construction will proceed as planned. Required documents are minimal — it is primarily a formal confirmation.

**Deadline**: at least 2 working days before the actual start date.

### 3. Gereedmelding — 10 working days after completion

The gereedmelding is filed after construction is complete and includes the **dossier bevoegd gezag** — the full evidence package proving the building meets regulations. This is the big one.

The dossier bevoegd gezag typically contains:

- All inspection reports from the kwaliteitsborger
- The verklaring kwaliteitsborger (formal declaration that the building complies)
- Resolution evidence for every finding that was flagged during inspections
- Valid certificates (fire safety, structural calculations, energy performance)
- The borgingsplan and risicobeoordeling
- Photos and measurement records

**Deadline**: within 10 working days after the oplevering date. The building cannot be legally occupied until the gemeente has received the complete dossier.

## What goes wrong in practice

Most contractors discover too late that their dossier is incomplete. Common problems:

- **Missing certificates** — a supplier promised a CE declaration but never sent it
- **Expired certificates** — a fire safety certificate expired during construction
- **Unresolved findings** — the kwaliteitsborger flagged issues that were never formally closed with evidence
- **Lost documents** — inspection reports buried in email threads
- **Deadline surprise** — the gereedmelding deadline arrives and the dossier is 60% complete

The result: project delays, rushed filing, and liability exposure that lasts 10 years.
