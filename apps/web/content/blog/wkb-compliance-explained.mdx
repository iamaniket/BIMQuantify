The **Wet kwaliteitsborging voor het bouwen** (Wkb) — the Dutch Quality Assurance in Building Act — went into effect on January 1, 2024. It shifts responsibility for building quality from municipalities to private quality assurance firms (*kwaliteitsborgers*) and increases the contractor's liability.

## What changed for contractors

Before Wkb, municipalities checked whether a building met the *Bouwbesluit* during the permit process. Now, for consequence class 1 buildings (houses, smaller commercial buildings), the **contractor bears primary responsibility** for building quality.

Key changes for the aannemer:

- **Builder liability** — the contractor is liable for defects discovered after handover, unless they can prove the defect was not their fault.
- **Three mandatory filings** — bouwmelding (4 weeks before start), informatieplicht (2 working days before start), and gereedmelding (10 working days after completion). Missing any of these can halt your project.
- **Dossier bevoegd gezag** — a formal evidence file proving the finished building meets regulations must be submitted to the municipality before the building can be occupied.
- **Kwaliteitsborger required** — an independent quality assurer must be contracted before construction starts.

## The dossier challenge

The dossier bevoegd gezag is the biggest paperwork challenge for contractors under Wkb. It requires:

- All inspection reports from the kwaliteitsborger
- Resolution evidence for every finding
- Valid certificates (fire safety, structural, energy)
- The borgingsplan and risicobeoordeling
- The verklaring kwaliteitsborger at completion

Most contractors manage this across email, shared drives, and spreadsheets. Documents get lost, certificates expire unnoticed, and the deadline for gereedmelding arrives before the dossier is complete.
